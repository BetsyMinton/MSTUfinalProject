Solids or Faces?

There are two ways to model in 3D. The simplest to learn for many people is to start with solid shapes and combine them to get the finished product. TinkerCad uses this process. In this example the house is made up of a rectangular prism and a triangular prism.

The professional software starts with sketches. Those 2-dimensional shapes or faces are then extruded into 3-dimensional shapes. This is a more powerful option but can be difficult for beginners to understand. Programs such as Fusion360 offer free licenses for students and eductators to use.
